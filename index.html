Nicole Comfort

PhD Candidate

Environmental Health Sciences

Mailman School of Public Health

Columbia University


Nicole received a BS in neuroscience from Northeastern University in 2015. She is currently working at the Laboratory of Precision Environmental Health under the mentorship of Dr. Andrea Baccarelli to study extracellular vesicles as biomarkers of environmental exposures and disease.

Her research generally involves the analysis of RNA-sequencing data. Recent projects have focused on a longitudinal transcriptomic characterization of aging sciatic nerves in sarcopenia and on plasma extracellular vesicle microRNA as biomarkers of cognitive decline in a cohort of elderly men in Boston, MA.

In her spare time, Nicole enjoys walking her two dogs, playing video and board games, traveling, dining out, and painting. You can check out some of her commissioned pet portraits here.
